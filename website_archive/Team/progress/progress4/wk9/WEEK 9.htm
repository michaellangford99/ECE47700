Description of design efforts:

This week was all about cleaning up the SPI code (getting the LSM6DSL chip to give clean, non-interference signals). I started with getting a new STEVAL-MKI78V2 LSM6DSL developer board from the lab and hooking it up to my STM32F411RE Nucleo board with my previously written code. I just tried to read the WHO_AM_I register and got a value of 0x40 on the MISO line from the LSM6DSL. This is incorrect as the WHO_AM_I register is supposed to give a fixed value of 0x6A. After some research, some people have been directly inputting an external voltage of 1.8V to VDDIO to the chip. After trying this, the correct hex value of 0x6A was now being read. This is shown with a slightly different electrical setup (explained below Figure 1) in Figure 1.

Figure 1: AD2 readout from the WHO_AM_I register

The interesting part of this was that VDDIO was supposed to come up either at the same time as VDD or after VDD, so having an external chip power it at a lower voltage wouldn’t make too much sense for it to be working. To counteract this, instead of using a separate power supply, 2 diodes were placed in series between VDD and VDDIO which brought the voltage being supplied to VDDIO down to ~1.9V. I also placed a 10k pullup resistor on MISO and tied SCx and SDx on the chip to VDDIO. These changes didn’t affect the output at all, other than having MISO idle high, but I didn’t want any more electrical problems to even potentially occur while coding the SPI again. All these electrical changes are shown below in Figure 2.

Figure 2: The electrical setup of the STEVAL-MKI78V2 LSM6DSL developer board

I had to fiddle with the initialization of the chip to get the stable readout that I wanted from the gyroscope and accelerometer, which included trying to use the chips FIFO readout and interrupt-based reading, however, both these resulted in less stable readouts anyways, so I played with the more simple accelerometer and gyroscope control based parameters and found the settings that best fit getting a stable output. Figure 4 is showing the initialization code with the parameters that worked best (to see what settings are used look at [1]). Shown in Figure 5 and 6 is a simple readable example of the OUTX_L_G register (the low byte gyroscope X read register) before and after moving the chip.
